ASV Validation & Thermal Regulation

Validating autonomous surface vessel performance through thermal modeling and environmental stress testing.

1. Hull Evolution & Fluid-Structure Design

Before achieving operational status, the vessel underwent several hull iterations to optimize for stability and internal volume. Early designs focused on displacement, while later iterations refined the aluminum chassis interface to improve the seal for sensitive electronics and maximize surface area contact with the water for cooling.

2. Thermal Regulation & Heat Transfer

Operating high-draw electronics (Jetson GPUs) in a sealed environment risks thermal runaway. I am conducting a transient thermal analysis to understand how the system approaches steady-state temperatures.

$$ \rho V C_p \frac{dT}{dt} = \dot{Q}_{gen} - h A (T_{int} - T_{ext}) $$

Thermistor Integration: To validate the model, I integrated a network of thermistors at critical junctions (CPU, Battery, and Internal Air). This allows for real-time data acquisition to correlate the Convective Heat Transfer Coefficient ($h$) of the water-cooled aluminum walls with theoretical predictions.

The goal is to determine the Time to Critical Temperature to define mission duration limits.

Thermal Resistance Circuit

3. Environmental Stress: Waterproofing

Integrity in a maritime environment is binary: it is either watertight or a failure. I led waterproofing testing across three scenarios:

  • Static Submersion: Validating gasket compression under constant hydrostatic pressure.
  • Dynamic Splash/Washout: Simulating wave crashing and high-speed turns where water is forced against seams.
  • Thermal Cycling: Testing if internal pressure changes (due to heat) compromise the seals.
