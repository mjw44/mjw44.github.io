Problem & Approach

Design Requirements

ExxonMobil required a variable-height structure to dynamically position hydrogen sensors up to 30 feet during field testing. The system demanded strict survival margins under transient wind loads and high-pressure discharges.

I led the mechanical architecture from concept to delivery. This included managing the BOM, selecting stiffness-optimized profiles, and coordinating with the electrical team to integrate ruggedized sensor networks.

Thrust Loading — First Principles

Before selecting profiles, I evaluated if the high-pressure hydrogen jet generated thrust loads capable of compromising structural stability. Using 1D conservation of momentum at the nozzle exit, I derived the net reaction force.

Calculations proved the localized thrust was statistically negligible compared to wind drag and self-weight. Consequently, it was excluded from governing load cases to streamline optimization.

Euler Buckling — Critical Load

At full 30-foot extension, elastic column buckling was the primary failure mode. I mapped Euler buckling formulations across each telescopic segment, accounting for pinned-fixed and fixed-free joint boundary conditions.

While nominal profiles survived static axial loads, dynamic wind reactions introduced severe bending stress. To maximize flexural rigidity affordably, I implemented a back-to-back channel configuration, achieving a Factor of Safety of 2.0 across four design iterations.

Retrospective: What I Would Optimize

The prototype met all functional requirements and was delivered on schedule. A second iteration would target two optimizations:

01. Geometric Section Optimization

Reducing moment arms at the base connection would minimize peak bending moments, maximizing structural efficiency and allowing for lighter profiles.

02. Material Mass & Cost Reduction

While initial trade studies reduced costs by 15%, parts of the frame remained over-engineered. Algorithmic gauge-thickness optimization would safely minimize mass while maintaining strict safety bounds.
