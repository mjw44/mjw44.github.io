The Process

From Conceptualization to Design Review

The goal was to create a variable-height structure for testing hydrogen detection sensors to ensure safety during drill operations. The process involved rigorous Internal Design Reviews with the CEM director, Neill, where we balanced technical capability against strict budget constraints.

I managed the Bill of Materials (BOM) and coordinated with the electrical team to negotiate space for sensors and wiring within the structural footprint without compromising the 30-foot integrity.

Calculation Focus

Thrust & Control Volumes

One critical validation was the Thrust Calculation. Using a Control Volume analysis (FBD), I proved that the force exerted by the escaping hydrogen gas at high pressure was negligible relative to the structural rigidity.

Structural Integrity

Buckling Validation

Ensuring the structure could withstand environmental and operational loads meant calculating the Critical Buckling Load for the single-beam members. I performed iterative Second Moment of Inertia calculations to select the absolute minimum material required to safely support the span while meeting the "low cost" requirement.
