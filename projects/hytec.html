Technical Process

Conceptualization to Design Review

The project centered on creating a variable-height structure for testing hydrogen detection sensors. I managed the bill of materials and coordinated with the electrical team to ensure structural integrity across a 30-foot vertical span.

Iterative design reviews were conducted regularly with Mike, Austin, and Neill to ensure the project scope, criteria, and timeline were on track for the ExxonMobil delivery.

Physics Validation

Thrust & Control Volumes

I proved that the force exerted by escaping high-pressure hydrogen gas was negligible relative to the structural rigidity of the chosen profiles.

Structural Integrity

Buckling Validation

Ensuring the structure could withstand environmental and operational loads meant calculating the critical buckling load for the single-beam members.

The analysis verified that the factor of safety (FoS) remained within industrial standards even at the maximum 30-foot extension, preventing catastrophic failure from high-pressure discharge or wind loading.

Engineering Retrospective: Value Optimization

Reflecting on the initial design cycle, the primary area for growth lies in dimensional & fiscal optimization. While the prototype met all functional requirements, a secondary iteration would focus on two key engineering levers:

01. Geometric Efficiency

By aggressively iterating on component configurations, the overall length could be reduced to minimize moment arms and improve the system's stiffness-to-weight ratio.

02. Cost-Benefit Scaling

Further optimization of material volume would directly reduce manufacturing costs, moving toward a leaner industrial design.
