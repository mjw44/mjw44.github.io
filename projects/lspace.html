Passive Thermal Architecture

The Equilibrium Constraint

Operating without active refrigeration, the architecture had to prevent cold-soak failure at 120 K nights while rejecting 545 W of peak electrical heat during the day. The entire system was sized against a strict steady-state equilibrium model:

$$ Q_{gen} + Q_{solar} = Q_{rad} + Q_{stored} $$

Net heat flux must equal zero at operating thresholds

I sized each passive mechanism directly against this balance: insulation for nighttime heat retention, PCMs for daytime thermal mass, and louvers for variable radiation.

Control Mechanisms

Multilayer Insulation (MLI)

Wraps the chassis to severely restrict conductive and radiative heat loss during the 120 K night. Sized against the minimum survival temp of the most sensitive payload.

Phase Change Materials

Absorbs latent heat during peak operations. Acts as a thermal capacitor, buffering component temperature spikes without requiring active fluid loops.

Bimetallic Louvers

Provides autonomous, passive regulation of the rover's surface emissivity. Actuates open to reject $Q_{rad}$ under solar load, and closes to retain heat at night.

Thermal Trade Studies

Resistance vs. Mass Optimization

Iterated across insulation and structural profiles to optimize the critical trade-off: maximizing system thermal resistance while strictly adhering to the 175 kg total mass budget.

Trade Study: Material Selection Ledger

Cross-System Integration

Thermal boundaries constrained every other sub-system. I drove resolutions across four critical interface conflicts:

Mechanical: Thermal Breaks

Engineered thermal isolation at structural mounting points to eliminate parasitic conduction paths that would bypass the MLI envelope.

Electrical: Flux Mapping

Mapped transient power dissipation curves to size PCM mass against actual dynamic heat flux, rather than over-engineering for static worst-case estimates.

Science: Noise Isolation

Thermally decoupled sensitive instruments from the primary chassis to prevent conducted heat from introducing signal noise into temperature-dependent data.

CDH: Autonomous Shedding

Integrated TCS telemetry with Command & Data Handling to enable autonomous load-shedding when approaching thermal saturation limits.
