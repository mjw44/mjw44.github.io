Role & Mission

Why This Team Exists

LBR had no dedicated simulation and validation function before this role. The S&V team was founded to ensure every significant part goes through an analytical verification cycle before design freeze.

Scope

The team owns the full validation chain: identifying critical load cases, bounding the problem with hand calculations, executing FEA, and producing documentation that ties simulation to manufacturing and testing plans.

Validation Pipeline

Stage 01: Hand Calculations

First-principles analysis establishes the governing load case, critical cross-section, and expected failure mode before FEA is set up.

Stage 02: FEA

FEA resolves stress concentrations and geometric effects the hand calculation cannot capture. Results are checked against the analytical baseline.

Stage 03: Test Correlation

Physical testing plans are developed to correlate simulation outputs with real-world performance. Discrepancies refine the model for future parts.

Team Deliverables

Force Analysis

FBDs and maximum force derivations under worst-case conditions: cornering, braking, and impact.

Material Selection

Candidates evaluated against yield strength, fatigue life, machinability, and cost. Rationale is documented, not just the final choice.

Manufacturing Process

Manufacturing method effects on microstructure and residual stress are identified and their impact on part performance is quantified.

FEA Report

Mesh convergence, boundary condition justification, and FoS against yield and ultimate failure under the validated load case.

Case Study: Wheel Hub Spline

Laser Cutting and the Heat-Affected Zone

The wheel hub spline is a candidate for laser cutting. The concern: laser cutting introduces a heat-affected zone (HAZ) along the cut edge, where rapid heating and quench cooling produce a martensitic microstructure. This raises hardness but reduces ductility at the spline tooth flanks.

Under static load, increased hardness is beneficial. Under cyclic drivetrain torque, reduced ductility at the tooth root raises the risk of brittle crack initiation, where Kt is already highest.

Validation Question

The FEA applies maximum drivetrain torque and resolves stress at the tooth root. The check is whether Kt · σnom exceeds the fatigue limit of the hardened HAZ material, not the base material. If it does, the geometry is modified to reduce Kt or laser cutting is replaced with a process that avoids HAZ formation.
