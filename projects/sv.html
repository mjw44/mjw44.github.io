Role & Mission

Why This Team Exists

The Simulation & Validation team was established to make analytical verification a standard part of the design cycle. Rather than relying on intuition alone, critical components are reviewed through bounded assumptions, first-principles analysis, simulation, and documented engineering judgment.

Scope

The role spans the full validation chain: defining governing load cases, checking geometry against functional constraints, building FEA models, and translating results into decisions that support manufacturing, testing, and design iteration.

Validation Pipeline

Stage 01: Hand Calculations

First-principles analysis establishes load paths, expected failure modes, and the baseline checks that frame the simulation work.

Stage 02: Geometry & FEA

Geometry-sensitive behavior is studied with simulation to capture stress concentrations, boundary-condition effects, and configuration tradeoffs.

Stage 03: Design Decisions

Results are compiled into clear technical documentation so design changes can be justified, communicated, and carried forward into testing.

Team Deliverables

Force Analysis

Free-body diagrams and bounding calculations that define the governing conditions for validation work.

Geometry Review

Evaluation of whether a proposed configuration supports the intended load path, packaging, and functional requirements.

Simulation Reports

Documented assumptions, boundary conditions, and results that connect the model to the design decision being made.

Design Guidance

Engineering recommendations on material choice, geometry revision, and next-step testing priorities.
