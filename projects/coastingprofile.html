Cadillac LYRIQ Coasting Profile

Predictive Modeling for ECOCAR V2X

Project Overview

As part of the ECOCAR V2X challenge, I developed a model designed to predict the coasting profile of the Cadillac LYRIQ. By translating vehicle dynamics into a computational loop in SIMULINK, I solved differential equations that account for aerodynamic drag, rolling friction, and gravitational forces. This allowed braking optimization and timing enhancements.

Mathematical Modeling

The core of the project involved modeling the vehicle as a dynamic system governed by Newton’s Second Law. To achieve predictive accuracy, the model accounts for the non-linear nature of aerodynamic drag alongside gravitational components determined by road geoemetry.

Governing Equation:

$$ \sum F = ma \implies -[C_d(v)v^2 + \mu g \cos(\theta) + g \sin(\theta)] = m \frac{dv}{dt} $$

Experimental Validation

Validation was conducted through a two-stage testing protocol. Initial simulations (Test 1) focused on isolating aerodynamic drag to establish a baseline acceleration-velocity relationship. In the second phase (Test 2), I introduced friction forces and angular road constraints to simulate real-world conditions.

Engineering Takeaways

  • Recursive Solving: Developed an iterative loop in Simulink to solve second-order differential equations based on real-time feedback of drag and friction forces.
  • Parameter Sensitivity: By isolating air drag from rolling resistance, I determined that aerodynamic efficiency is the dominant factor in LYRIQ range at speeds exceeding 45 mph.
  • V2X Application: This kinematic model allows the vehicle to approximate when to lift off the accelerator to arrive at a green light without braking.

Retrospective: Engineering Growth

Looking back on the development cycle, there are several architectural and procedural changes I would implement for a higher quality result:

  • Non-Linear Friction Coefficients: Tire deformation and heat build-up make friction non-linear.
  • Data Version Control (Git): I lost the final iteration of this project. This event calls for a better organization of data and information.
  • Experimental Cross-Referencing: I would have liked to cross-reference these Simulink results with actual CAN bus data from the LYRIQ to tune coefficients.
