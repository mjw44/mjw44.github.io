Cadillac LYRIQ Coasting Profile

Predictive Modeling of Vehicle Kinematic Decay for ECOCAR V2X

Project Overview

As part of the ECOCAR V2X challenge, I developed a high-fidelity model designed to predict the coasting profile of the Cadillac LYRIQ. By translating vehicle dynamics into a computational loop, I solved differential equations that account for aerodynamic drag, rolling friction, and gravitational forces. This tool allows for the precise estimation of vehicle deceleration, providing a critical foundation for optimizing regenerative braking strategies.

Mathematical Modeling

The core of the project involved modeling the vehicle as a dynamic system governed by Newton’s Second Law. To achieve predictive accuracy, the model accounts for the non-linear nature of aerodynamic drag alongside gravitational components determined by road gradients.

Governing Equation:

$$ \sum F = ma \implies -[C_d(v)v^2 + \mu g \cos(\theta) + g \sin(\theta)] = m \frac{dv}{dt} $$

Experimental Validation

Validation was conducted through a two-stage testing protocol. Initial simulations (Test 1) focused on isolating aerodynamic drag to establish a baseline acceleration-velocity relationship. In the second phase (Test 2), I introduced friction forces and angular road constraints to simulate real-world conditions.

Engineering Takeaways

  • Recursive Solving: Utilized iterative loops to solve differential equations regarding drag, friction, and gravity.
  • Component Isolation: Isolated drag and friction forces to verify the sensitivity of the Cadillac LYRIQ’s coasting distance to specific environmental variables.
  • V2X Application: This model serves as a prerequisite for predictive "Green Wave" driving, where the vehicle calculates optimal coasting to minimize energy loss at upcoming intersections.
