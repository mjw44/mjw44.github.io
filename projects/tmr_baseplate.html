Design Constraints & Material Selection

The objective was to develop a baseplate for the TMR Autonomous Surface Vessel to house sensitive observation electronics. The design was governed by real-world constraints: low cost, simple geometry for quick manufacturing at Texas Inventionworks (TIW), and easy assembly.

Material Logic: Acrylic (PMMA) was selected for its high strength relative to wood and its isotropic, predictable behavior compared to 3D-printed polymers. Using 1/4" stock allowed us to maximize stiffness while maintaining a low profile.

Technical Workflow: Analytical Derivations

The following sequence documents the transition from raw environmental data to finalized mechanical specifications.

1. Maximum Force Loads
2. Thickness Determination
3. Centripetal Acceleration
4. Dynamic Loading (Acceleration)
5 & 6. Bearing Stress Analysis
7. Revised Torque Specs

Verification & Prototyping

I utilized FEA to identify stress concentrations (Kt) that hand calculations might overlook. The first physical prototype used aluminum bars to reinforce the interface.

Academic Extension: Fracture Mechanics

To expand the project's breadth, future iterations will focus on fracture mechanics. While Goodman ensures operation within a safe envelope, LEFM would allow us to quantify crack initiation and propagation (da/dN) at stress concentrations.

Next Steps

We will continue to create prototypes and test out the baseplate. When coming up with the final design, fracture mechanics will be utilized to lock in material selection, and to know when maintenance should be conducted (crack propagation).
