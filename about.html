About

Julian Whiteley.

I am a mechanical engineering honors student at UT Austin focused on the places where hardware has to prove itself: structural analysis, thermal systems, simulation, validation, and system integration.

My work has moved between research, product engineering, and student-led design teams. I have designed and validated a 30-foot hydrogen release structure at the Center for Electromechanics, founded a simulation and validation team for Longhorn Baja Racing, led mechanical systems work for Texas Marine Robotics, and built thermal-control projects that connect models to measured behavior.

I like engineering that has consequences in the real world. Before I trust a model, I want to understand the load path, the heat flow, the assumptions, and the failure mode.

Current Work

At Integra, I am engineering a closed-loop fluid flow testing system and developing hydrostatic pressure testing protocols to validate structural integrity under operational loads. I also wrote a SolidWorks VBA macro that removed more than 100 hours of manual engineering overhead across 2,000+ legacy files.

In parallel, I lead simulation and validation work for Longhorn Baja Racing, where my team connects first-principles analysis, FEA, strain gauge data, and physical testing into one design workflow.

Mindset

Failure is the only reliable teacher. I approach problems by deriving from first principles before reaching for a simulation, and I hold myself to the same standard I would apply to a design: if it cannot be justified, it gets revised.
