About

Julian Whiteley.

I am a mechanical engineering student at UT Austin with a focus on structural analysis, simulation, and systems integration. My work spans FEA, thermal systems, and dynamics, grounded in first-principles reasoning and validated against physical reality.

I want to pursue graduate research and contribute to energy systems and product development where the engineering problems are genuinely unsolved. In the meantime, I tutor mechanics of solids, statics, and dynamics because teaching something is the fastest way to find out whether you actually understand it.

Goals
Graduate Degree

Pursuing an MS or PhD in mechanical engineering to work on problems that require more than an undergraduate toolkit.

Energy

Structural and thermal systems for energy infrastructure, from conventional to emerging technologies.

Product Design

Building physical products that work reliably in the real world, designed from first principles and validated through testing.

Mindset

Failure is the only reliable teacher. I approach problems by deriving from first principles before reaching for a simulation, and I hold myself to the same standard I'd apply to a design: if it can't be justified, it gets revised.
